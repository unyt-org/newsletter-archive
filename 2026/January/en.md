# unyt.org Looks Back: 2025 – A Year of Transformation

Dear readers,

In this January edition of the unyt.org newsletter, we would like to look back on the past year. At first glance, one might notice that development progress in our main projects UIX and DATEX Core JS (Legacy) has slowed down. In fact, for the past year our focus has been on developing the new [DATEX Core](https://github.com/unyt-org/datex-core) in Rust.

With over 120,000 lines of Rust code in this area alone, we are working intensively to ensure that our new [data transmission and communication system](https://datex.unyt.org) can serve as a reliable, standardized, and production-ready foundation for an entirely new version of UIX in the future – and beyond that, will run on many additional device types, [including embedded devices](https://github.com/unyt-org/datex-core-embedded). Of course, along the way, UIX 0.x and DATEX Core JS (Legacy) will continue to receive important bug fixes and maintenance updates.

# Development

The year 2025 was characterized by foundational developments that lay the groundwork for the next generation of our technologies. The focus was on the new DATEX Core in Rust as well as creating a robust ecosystem of tools and integrations.

## [DATEX Core](https://github.com/unyt-org/datex-core)

With **[DATEX Core Rust](https://github.com/unyt-org/datex-core)**, we have created an entirely new foundation for the DATEX protocol. The key milestones of the year were:

* **Protocol Version 2 Foundations** – The foundations for the second generation of the DATEX protocol were developed and implemented.

* **August 5: First Block Transmission** – On this day, blocks were successfully transmitted over the DATEX protocol for the first time – a historic milestone for the project.

* **JavaScript-Native Communication** – Also in August, the first successful communication between a JavaScript environment and a native endpoint via WebSocket was achieved.

* **Type System** – A complete type system was established that forms the basis for type-safe DATEX communication.

* **DIF Protocol** – The DATEX Interchange Format (DIF) enables data exchange between different DATEX implementations and external environments such as JavaScript.

* **New Programming Language** – In December, development of a first version of the DATEX cross-network programming language was completed.

* **[Embedded Support](https://github.com/unyt-org/datex-core-embedded)** – In September, DATEX was ported to ESP32 microcontrollers for the first time, opening up new application areas in the IoT sector.

* **Native CLI Tools** – A native command-line tool was developed that provides REPL functionality and direct execution of DX files.

## [DATEX Workbench](https://github.com/unyt-org/datex-workbench)

The **DATEX Workbench** was designed as a new tooling and debugging environment for the DATEX ecosystem:

* **DATEX Pointer View / Explorer** – Enables inspection of a DATEX endpoint's memory.
* **DATEX Block Viewer** – Allows intercepting and inspecting incoming and outgoing DXB blocks.

## [UIX](https://github.com/unyt-org/uix)

UIX received continuous maintenance and care throughout the year:

* **Bug Fixes** – Numerous bug fixes ensured a more stable user experience.
* **Maintenance Updates** – Ongoing maintenance work kept the framework current and compatible.

# Vision 2026

For the year 2026, we have already established a plan that we will present at our public Annual General Assembly on **February 14, 2026** at **11:00 AM** CET.

In particular, our Vision 2026 includes:

* **DATEX Core Prototype** – A first reliably usable public prototype of the new DATEX Core with fine-tuned language semantics.
* **DATEX Core JS** – A first release of our JavaScript bindings for the new DATEX Core.
* **UIX Next-Gen Prototype** – The next UIX generation, which unifies DATEX with a full-stack web framework, providing reliable cross-network reactivity, backend imports, and DOM rendering.
